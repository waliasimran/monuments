The RED FORT Complex was built as the palace fort of Shahjahanabad – the new capital of the fifth Mughal Emperor of India, Shah Jahan. Named for its massive enclosing walls of red sandstone, it is adjacent to an older fort, the Salimgarh, built by Islam Shah Suri in 1546, with which it forms the Red Fort Complex. The private apartments consist of a row of pavilions connected by a continuous water channel, known as the Nahr-i-Behisht (Stream of Paradise). The LOTUS TEMPLE in New Delhi is one of 7 temples of the Baha'i faith in the world. Like all Baha'i temples its structure is a circular 9-sided shape. The building is made up of 27 free-standing marble petals arranged to form the 9 sides of the temple. The central worship hall inside is 40m high and has capacity for 2,500 people. The temple and its architect have won several awards – including one from the UK's Institute of Structural Engineers in 1987 for producing a building 'so striking in its visual impact.'
